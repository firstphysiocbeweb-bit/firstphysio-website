Pain Management Program

Most Popular

There is no question that pain is essential to our survival. With an acute injury or illness, pain is the body's way of letting the mind know that there is something wrong. If pain persists past the acute and into the chronic stage, it often ceases to be a useful warning. Ongoing pain may cause problems with friends and loved ones, feelings of isolation, anxiety, depression, insomnia, and fear of activity.

Our Approach:

  • Working out the amount of activity and exercise you can do without increasing pain
  • Improving general strength and fitness with exercises and stretches
  • Reducing effects of stress and tension through relaxation techniques
  • Reducing and managing flare-ups through personalized management plan
  • Assisting with return to activities using graded activity and goal setting

Treatment Includes:

  • Education (posture, pacing, disease, remaining active)
  • Exercise (aerobic, strengthening, stretching, motor control)
  • Manual therapy (joint mobilization and manipulation)
  • Electrical stimulation (TENS, interferential current, Microcurrent)
  • Heat modalities (hot packs, ultrasound)
  • Cold modalities (ice pack, ice bath)

Arthritis Management Program

Joint Health

Arthritis is a joint disorder causing inflammation in one or more joints. It is common in adults 65 and older, but can affect people of all ages. Physiotherapy is key to the treatment and management of arthritis, focusing on the reduction of pain and stiffness as well as increasing movement and range of motion.

Arthritis doesn't mean you have to live with pain and limitations. Our specialized program helps maintain joint mobility, reduce pain, and improve your quality of life through targeted exercises and manual therapy.

Treatment Modalities:

  • Joint Mobilisation
  • Exercise Prescription
  • Electrotherapy Modalities
  • Traction
  • Frequency Specific Microcurrent
  • Myofascial Release & Trigger Point Therapy

Additional Support:

  • Supports/aids recommendation
  • Footwear modification
  • Patient education
  • Home exercise program
  • Maintenance physiotherapy visits (every 4-6 weeks)
  • Weight management guidance

Conditions We Treat:

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • Ankylosing spondylitis
  • Gout
  • Degenerative joint disease

Treatment Includes:

  • Joint mobility exercises
  • Strengthening weak muscles
  • Pain relief modalities
  • Hot/cold therapy
  • Activity modification training
  • Assistive device training

Post-Operative Rehabilitation

Surgery Recovery

After your surgery it is important to begin the rehabilitation process as soon as possible to ensure that the procedure is successful and your recovery is optimised. Following any operation you are left with general muscle weakness, swelling, scar tissue and adhesions and a reduced range of motion.

We Support Recovery From:

  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) reconstruction
  • Hip and knee replacements
  • Rotator cuff repairs
  • Arthroscopes
  • Post Fracture

Our Approach:

  • Hands-on individually tailored treatments
  • Exercise-based rehabilitation regime
  • Pain & swelling management
  • Range of motion exercises
  • Progressive strengthening
  • Return-to-activity planning

Senior Fitness Program

Age 50+

Senior's Fitness Program is for mature adults 50+, offering a comprehensive fitness facility designed exclusively for the mature adult. The program also includes a physiotherapy regimen to relieving pain and addresses those functional limitations particular to members with chronic conditions. Remember: "You don't stop moving because you get old. You get old because you stop moving!"

Program Goals:

  • Improve the Health of the Aging
  • Lengthen Independence
  • Improve Overall Fitness
  • Reduce Falls
  • Increase Activities of Daily Living (ADLs)
  • Improve the quality of life

Risk Factors Addressed:

  • Biological: Mobility problems, muscle weakness, balance issues
  • Chronic health conditions: arthritis, stroke, vision changes
  • Behavioral: Inactivity, medication side effects
  • Environmental: Home hazards, incorrect use of assistive devices
  • Balance training exercises
  • Education to reduce risk factors at home

Parkinson's Program

Specialized Neuro

Parkinson disease is a progressive disorder of the nervous system that affects a person's movement. People with Parkinson's disease move more slowly, tend to take tiny steps, and may freeze in place. The Physiotherapist will tailor the program to the individual to help improve trunk rotation, balance, increase speed, and have a better quality of life.

Symptoms We Help With:

  • Gait Disorders (reduced arm swing, short-stride, shuffling gait, freezing)
  • Postural Lean
  • Tremor
  • Rigidity & Stiffness
  • Bradykinesia (slow movements)
  • Non-motor Symptoms (micrographia, swallowing difficulties)

Services Offered:

  • Personal evaluation of Parkinson's symptoms
  • Individualized exercise programs targeting symptoms
  • Gait training & postural exercises
  • Stretching & strengthening
  • Cardiovascular & balance exercise programs
  • Parkinson's disease information & education

Sports Injury Management and Rehab

Athletes

Sport provides us with exercise which is essential for promoting good health. Sport may also result in injuries, many of which can be prevented. The goal of First Physio is to TREAT, REHABILITATE, RECONDITION and return the physically active population to their prior activity level. Remember "Prevention is always better than cure".

Prevention Tips:

  • Ensure adequate warm up and warm down exercises
  • Ensure you are fit enough for your competition level
  • Have adequate knowledge of the sport
  • ALWAYS HEAR YOUR BODY - treat minor injuries immediately
  • HYDRATE REGULARLY

Techniques Used:

  • Functional Activities
  • Electrotherapeutic Modality
  • Manual Therapy & Footwear Modification
  • Taping & Bracing
  • Frequency Specific Microcurrent (FSM)
  • Proprioception & Sports-specific training

Corporate Physio Solutions (Ergotherapy)

Workplace Health

Successful companies expect their employees to perform well. Work-related stress symptoms have a negative effect on performance. Our Goal is to provide your corporate team with the strategies, intervention and tools to lead them towards improved health, wellbeing and overall productivity. Injury Prevention:

Biomechanical Assessment

Movement Analysis

Biomechanics is the study of movement and the interaction between body parts. Our physiotherapist is trained in the assessment of sporting biomechanics - swimming stroke, tennis serve, golfing, running or kicking technique. Often the solution to lingering pains or recurrent injuries stems from the correction of underlying incorrect technique.

Assessment Includes:

  • Swimming stroke analysis
  • Tennis serve assessment
  • Golfing technique
  • Running/kicking technique
  • Identify abnormal movement patterns
  • Pin point the cause of the problem

Benefits:

  • More efficient pattern of movement
  • Improved performance
  • Better results
  • Prevention of injuries
  • Correction of underlying incorrect technique
  • Address cause of recurrent injuries

Program Benefits:
