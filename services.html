Our Services

Comprehensive Physiotherapy Services

Advanced treatment techniques using a personalized, multimodal approach to your care.

Our Three-Pillar Approach

At First Physio, we categorize our treatments into three comprehensive pillars: Protective, Restorative, and Locomotive therapy. This holistic approach ensures we address every aspect of your recovery.

Protective Therapy

Protecting your body while promoting healing and preventing further injury

Moist Heat Therapy

Increases tissue extensibility, blood flow and decreases stiffness using hydrocollator packs. Reduces muscle spasms and prepares muscles for stretches and exercises.

Cold Therapy

Decreases inflammation and pain post exercise/therapy. Helps with muscle strains, ligament sprains, and post injury or surgery pain relief.

Ergonomics Consultancy

Modifying workstation and office layout to prevent repetitive strain injuries. Helps with back pain, wrist pain, and headaches from desk work.

Patient Education

Prevention is better than cure. We educate you on optimal lifestyle practices, disease prevention, and injury management for lifelong health.

Posture Assessment

We assess to realign your bones. Poor posture causes back pain, neck pain, headaches, and RSI injuries. Get significant improvements in pain and function.

Restorative Therapy

Restoring function, reducing pain, and accelerating healing

Deep Tissue Massage

Loosens muscle-related trigger points and tight myofascial/connective tissue. Breaks up scar tissue from previous injuries, relieves pain and reduces stress.

Chest Physiotherapy

Maximizes lung function and capacity for efficient breathing. Helps with COPD, chronic asthma, emphysema, cystic fibrosis, and bronchitis.

Ultrasound Therapy

Stimulates cell activity and blood flow for accelerated healing. Generates heat within body parts to increase circulation and promote tissue healing.

Phonophoresis Therapy

Applies topical medications through the skin to treat soft tissue injuries and inflammatory conditions using ultrasound technology.

Electrical Stimulation (NMES)

Stimulates muscles at different intensities of electrical current. Includes TENS, Interferential Current, and Hi-Volt for pain relief and muscle strengthening.

Frequency Specific Microcurrent

Our unique way of treating nerve and muscle pain. Uses very low voltage micro amperage current to stimulate the body's healing process.

Joint Mobilization

Increases and maintains joint mobility. Uses oscillating or traction force to promote movement in stagnant tissues and joints.

Manual Therapy

Hands-on physiotherapy treatment for pain relief, increased range of motion, and decreased inflammation. Includes joint manipulation and soft tissue massage.

Neuro-muscular Re-education

Improves balance, coordination, and posture through repetitive movements, posturing, and stimulation designed to reinforce nerve signals.

Postural Training

Better posture allows better movement and prevents injury. 90% of musculoskeletal pain problems present with less-than-ideal posture.

Soft Tissue Release

Myofascial release therapy for treating skeletal muscle immobility and pain. Relaxes contracted muscles, improves circulation, and stimulates stretch reflex.

Kinesio & McConnell Taping

Mechanical support to facilitate correct movement patterns. Decreases pain, overuse, inflammation, and increases postural awareness.

Locomotive/Movement Therapy

Restoring movement, building strength, and getting you back to life

Balance & Proprioceptive Training

Balance is crucial for effective motion. Teaches your body to control position of deficient or injured joints using balance board exercises.

Gait/Ambulation Training

Helps individuals resume normal walking following injury, illness or surgery. Access to the most innovative techniques and equipment in walking therapy.

Specialised Exercise Therapy

Combines strength, stamina, stability and sensory training for overall movement therapy. Individualized programs to improve health, function and well-being.

Strength Training (PRE)

Progressive Resistance Exercise to increase strength of weak or injured muscles. Uses resistance bands, free weights, and body weight exercises.

Stretching & Flexibility

Improves muscle and soft tissue extensibility, prevents swelling and pain. Benefits athletes, housewives, elderly, and those with arthritis.

Range of Motion Exercises

Improves joint range of motion for normal movements. Includes Passive (PROM), Active Assistive (AAROM), and Active Range of Motion (AROM).

Myotherapy

Helps relax tight muscles and maintain muscle length. Uses hands-on manual techniques and massage to release muscle tension and trigger points.

Our Expertise

Advanced Treatment Techniques

McKenzie Method

Based on cause and effect relationship between pain behavior and repeated movements. Emphasizes patient education and self-treatment.

Maitland Method

Continuous evaluation and assessment of applied hands-on techniques and movements to identify problem areas and direct treatment.

Mulligan Concept

Manual therapy approach applying manual pressure to a stiff area while the patient is stretching. Restores motion and alleviates pain.

Core Stabilization

Core strengthening for trunk and hip muscles that provide a bracing effect to the spine, reducing stress and pain.
